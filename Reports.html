Laboratory Reports

Over the course of my undergraduate degree, I was required to under taken a 'Practical Physics' module as part of my MSci in Theoretical Physics. This involved spending numerous hours in the Blackett Laboratory undertaking various investigation in the fields of classical mechanics, optical physics, thermodynamics and more. The module also involved a computational aspect that consited of using programming languages such as Python to research more complex chaotic physical systems. These investigations required the write up of practical reports that can be found below. 

Filipovich L. C., Investigating Legendre Polynomial Solutions of the Simple Pendulum, Imperial College London, Blackett Laboratory, 1st Year Laboratory Report, December 2019

Filipovich L. C., A Quantitative Analysis of One-Dimensional N-slit Fraunhofer Diffraction, Imperial College London, Blackett Laboratory, 1st Year Laboratory Report, Febuary 2020

Filipovich L. C., Investigating the Mercury Lamp Yellow Spectral Line Using Computational Fourier Interferometry, Imperial College London, Blackett Laboratory, 2nd Year Laboratory Report, October 2020

Filipovich L. C., Simulating the Kinetic Theory of Gases using Object-Oriented Programming, Imperial College London, Blackett Laboratory, 2nd Year Laboratory Report, December 2020
